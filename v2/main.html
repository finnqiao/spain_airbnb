Barcelona Landmarks


Barcelona is a city marked by a rich history and unique culture. Notable landmarks include:

  • La Sagrada Familia, Antoni Gaudis premiere cathedral, which has been under construction since 1882 and is scheduled to be completed in 2032.
  • La Rambla, a street in central Barcelona, filled with music, restaurants, shops, and arts. It is located near the Gothic Quarter, the beach, and the neighborhood of El Raval, which hosts some of Gaudis more notable architectural works.
  • The Camp Nou stadium is home to FC Barcelona and is the third largest stadium in Europe, with a capacity of 99,354 people. It is a popular venue for sporting events and concerts.

Events in Barcelona


Mobile World Congress is the world's largest exhibition for the mobile industry, boasting approximately 100,000 attendees from all over the world. It is held at the Fira Gran Via in Southwest Barcelona.

Sonar is an electronic music festival held in Barcelona every year since 1994, growing to draw upwards of 120,000 attendees in recent years. The 2018 lineup included Diplo, the Gorillaz and LCD Soundsystem

Madrid Landmarks


Madrid, as the capital of Spain, is filled with many historic sites and national attractions. Notable landmarks include:

  • The Royal Palace of Madrid, the home of the Spanish royal family within the city, is located near the center of the city and is an excellent example of Baroque architecture.
  • The Museo Nacional del Prado is one of the premier art museums in the world. It features a plethora of works from the 12th to 20th centuries. Some notable acquisitions are Las Meninas by Diego Velzquez, The Garden of Earthly Delights by Hieronymous Bosch, and Third of May 1808 by Francisco Goya.
  • Santiago Bernabu Stadium, a stadium in northern Madrid, is home to Real Madrid Club de Ftbol. In addition to the large draw it has for all Real Madrid matches, it is also host to many larger tournaments and events.
